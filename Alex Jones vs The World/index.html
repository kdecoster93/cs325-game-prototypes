Alex Jones vs The World

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    • Alex Jones vs The World is a parody-based game where the player plays as the outrageous political commentator and conspiracy theorist Alex Jones in a post-apocalyptic coronavirus world. The originality of this game stems from merging 2D-Platformer and Dark Souls gameplay mechanics with a comedic setting and story. As the player tries to defeat enemies and bosses, they will listen to hilarious sound bites made by Alex Jones that are implemented in a way to give the game’s story context.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • Alex Jones vs The World utilizes an advanced moving system that allows the player to move in all 2-Dimensional X and Y directions. This includes the ability to walk, jump, and sprint quickly across the map. The game also has random sounds for every combat move and the pain recieved and inflicted by said moves. Alex Jones vs The World also includes a blood splatter system that spawns blood on enemy AI in different locations across their sprites body. All of this comes with additional tech that allows enemy AI to move towards the player and attack them in different and unique ways. Some of this AI code was made from foundational code adapted from a previous game made in class cited here Game Source
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    • I learned how to edit and create video cinematics in Adobe After Effects as well as incorporate them into the Phaser 3 engine.
    • I learned how to create a environmental objects that can react to player touch via tween animation.
    • I learned how to control when different music soundtracks play and end between scenes.
    • I learned how to make better AI for new and tougher enemies that could act similarly to the player in both movements and attacks.
    • I learned how to incorporate different background images to create a layering effect that made the composition of backgrounds seem to be one piece.
  • What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    • The hardest part in the process of making the final iteration was adding a sense of cinematic twists and turns to make the player feel as if there was rising action to a climax in the form of the final battle. I had to create four new enemies with two of them being different phases of the same boss. Each enemy required their own AI and animations as well as proper strategic placement throughout the levels. In order to weave the story of Alex Jones vs the World I had to make the later levels increase in difficulty while also trying to tell a story with minimal dialogue. After incorporating the cinematics and building the levels to depict a world with purpose, I feel comfortable saying there is a coherent story to my game.

  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?
    • The main game mechanics of Alex Jones vs The World are the fighting mechanics, platforming mechanics, and enemy AI. All three of these mechanics are important to the overall gameplay experience as either of the three can dull the enjoyment a player receives if one is subpar. The fighting mechanics will consist of close combat and projectile moves. The platforming mechanics will include 2D movement at various speeds and jumping. The enemy AI should challenge the player to master these controls, but not be so overbearing that it appears unfair.
  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?
    • Players should feel as if the controls are intuitive and natural. The fighting mechanics should provide visceral feedback via immediate audio and visual queues upon damage being dealt or taken. Each enemy AI type should serve a different role and be, individually, easy to deal with. In large groups they should work together to push the player to strategically eliminate them. Ultimately, the player should see the game as being a fair and challenging 2D fighting platformer that rewards and punishes the player for making the right and wrong decisions respectively. Additionally, the player should be amused and or laughing throughout their journey as they hear Alex Jones make ludicrous statements in response to the events occurring within the game.
  • Gameplay: A paragraph describing the actions the player can perform, the system dynamics, and the core mechanic. Include a concise explanation of the prototype’s inputs and their expected effects (how to play). You can also describe game play that is not in the prototype. You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
    • The gameplay consists of 3 major gameplay mechanics. The first mechanic is focused on movement which allows the player to move in any two-dimensional direction via walking, running, and jumping. The movement mechanic is critical to master in order to progress in the game when moving from one platform to another. The second mechanic is combat which gives the player the ability to do damage to enemies via punches and kicks while also including projectile based moves that allow the player to attack enemies from a distance with a finite amount of shots. The last gameplay mechanic is the enemy AI. Each different enemy that the player encounters will serve a different role in engaging the player and will have a different type of weakness. Together, different groups of enemies can pose a challenge to the player where one by themselves may not. All three of these gameplay mechanics will work in unison to drive the player forward into an engaging experience.

    • Alex Jones is an outrageous news commentator and conspiracy theorist that is known for his long-winded rants about globalists and the many conspiracies he supports and produces. Alex finds himself in the apocalyptic aftermath of the coronavirus wave that spread through America and the world at large.
