Meme Royale

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    • Meme Royale is a 2D Fighting Game where two players must battle each other in an arena until one is left standing. Unlike other fighting games, Meme Royale includes a health regeneration mechanic that allows players to strategically withdraw from battle in a temporary attempt to regain their footing before they continue to engage. This can create an interesting game of cat and mouse where a player that is skilled at moving can make a comeback by recovering from the damage they previously sustained. This game is also unique in the way that it uses popular memes from internet culture as its fighters.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • Meme Royale utilizes an advanced moving system that is more advanced then simple moving to the left right. This includes the ability to dash quickly across the map and behind opponents where they can be stricken. The game also has sounds for every combat move and the pain recieved by said moves. Meme Royale also includes a blood splatter system that spawns blood on each player in different locations across their sprites body. All of this comes with addiotional tech that allows two seperate people to battle on the same computer.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    • 1. I learned how to make a game that two players can both be invloved in with equal chances of winning and losing. 2. I learned how design and program fighting mechanics that feel appropriate for a 2D based fighter. 3. I learned how to send data from different scenes to others. 4. I learned how group sounds used for combat noises and build a system that plays random noises for each attack.
    What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    • The hardest part in the process of making this game was trying to balance each button that allowed player movement and animations. Buttons and animations often interfere with each other and create a hectic testing ground where you spend most of your time trying to get the right animation to play on a key press. Another difficult part of the game was learning how to make a sound system that could group and play random sounds for a single animation.
